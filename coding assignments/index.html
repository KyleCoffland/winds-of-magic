Magic in the Warhammer World

Magic is a deeply integral and important facet of the Warhammer setting, such that when the setting was remade into Age of Sigmar, the Winds of Magic became the literal foundation of the world itself. This website will provide an examination of this element of the setting.

Magic and Chaos

Magic is inextricably linked to Chaos, the otherworldly destructive forces which threaten every aspect of the world. This is why many civilizations and societies shun magic, with the mighty Empire of Sigmar outlawing the practice for almost all of its history. The power and utility which magic provides cannot be denied, however, and against the forces of Chaos, it is a weapon as indispensable as it is dangerous.

The Winds of Magic

Just as air moves throughout the world in the form of wind, so too do magical energies flow across the world in predictable patterns and currents. These currents are referred to as winds. At each of the planetâ€™s poles are massive wounds in reality, gateways into the nightmarish Realms of Chaos. The otherworldly energies of Chaos flow through these gates and into the material world, and these are magical energies. Upon entering the world, the raw magical energy splits into eight distinct currents; these are the Winds of Magic.

If left unchecked, the world would become so saturated with magical energy that it would effectively be absorbed into Realms of Chaos. To prevent this, the Elves in ancient times wove a Great Vortex into being which draws magical energy from across the planet and grounds it, keeping magical energy at a safe level. Of course, that this also empowers Elven magic was considered a fortunate by-product.

The Eight Winds

The eight winds consist of Ghur, Shyish, Aqshy, Azyr, Ghyran, Hysh, Chamon, and Ulgu. Each wind behaves in a different way, and fuels a particular practice, or lore, of magic.

Ghur, the Amber Wind

Ghur

Ghur is the brown or amber wind, and fuels the Lore of Beasts. Amber wizards are sometimes called Shamans.

Shyish, the Purple Wind

Shyish

Shyish is the purple wind, and fuels the Lore of Death. Amethyst wizards are sometimes called Spiriters, but NEVER Necromancers.

Aqshy, the Red Wind

Aqshy

Aqshy is the red wind, and fuels the Lore of Fire. Bright wizards are sometimes called Pyromancers.

Azyr, the Blue Wind

Azyr

Azyr is the blue wind, and fuels the Lore of the Heavens. Celestial wizards are sometimes called Astromancers.

Ghyran, the Green Wind

Ghyran

Ghyran is the green wind, and fuels the Lore of Life. Jade wizards are sometimes called Druids.

Hysh, the White Wind

Hysh

Hysh is the white wind, and fuels the Lore of Light. Light wizards are sometimes called Hierophants.

Chamon, the Yellow Wind

Chamon

Chamon is the yellow or gold wind, and fuels the Lore of Metal. Gold wizards are sometimes called Alchemists.

Ulgu, the Grey Wind

Ulgu

Ulgu is the grey wind, and fuels the Lore of Shadows. Grey wizards are sometimes called Shadowmancers.
